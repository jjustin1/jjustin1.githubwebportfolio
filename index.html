This is a fully Responsive web-portfolio Showcasing The Work of Justin Christopher Johnson

My name is Justin Johnson, and I created this online portfolio to showcase the various projects I have worked on throughout my early professional and academic career. My Projects (2016-2021)

Co-Founder & Senior Campaign Organizer: Organizing Black

Organizing Black is a grassroots member-led organization in Baltimore City that focuses on transformational direct action, political education, and participatory governance. Through these realms, we strive to build campaigns and programs that shift power and policy within Baltimore communities.

2021 Economic Policy Graduate Research

For my 2021 graduate research thesis, I used census data, spatial analytics software, and econometric methods to estimate the impact that single-family zoning restrictions have on segregation and inequality in the Baltimore Metropolitan Area.

  • Learn More

Maryland Organizing Lead: Poor People's Campaign June 2020 Mobilization Event

Worked with National Poor People's Campaign Maryland Chapter to coordinate field organizing infrastructure and tasks for the June 2020 National Mobilization Event, a digital action event and fundraiser that attracted over 2.5 million participants. As an organizing lead, I was responsible for coordinating community events and public outreach activities wtihin Maryland, as well as managing and producing social media content.

  • Learn More

Lead Field Coordinator & Research Assistant: Blueprint for Baltimore Research Campaign

The Blueprint for Baltimore was a collaborative research campaign that was designed to assess the political infrastructure of Baltimore City. By partnering with T.Rowe Price, OSI Baltimore, CASA Maryland, and other local organzations, we were able to gather and analyze data from over 10,000 Baltimore City residents. This data provided crucial information to researchers and policymakers about public opinion and the effect of various municipal programs and services throughout the City.

Baltimore Field Organizer: Black Census Project

The 2018 Black Census Project was the largest survey of Black people conducted in the United States since 1860.
